How does the max-width property help make images responsive?

The max-width property in CSS sets the maximum size that an element, such as an image, can be.
This is useful for making images responsive, as it prevents them from stretching beyond a certain limit on smaller screens.
Instead of changing the height, the image is scaled proportionally by reducing its width, thus maintaining its original appearance and adapting to different devices and screen sizes elegantly.
